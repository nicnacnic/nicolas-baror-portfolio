Seussical

With my successful design of Cinderella the year prior, Stage Left Children's Theater hired me once again to design their production of Seussical. This time around, the design process was a little different, as I got hired right at the start, and had the ability to add and change things as I desired.

The challenge this year was time. Being the only person on team lighting, it fell on me to hang, focus, and design the entire show. The limitations of the space didn't help; only having one way to get to each electric limited the amount of fixtures we could hang at once. With all that, the lighting department fell behind, with me doing as much as possible to get the rig up and running and programmed before tech began.

Despite the limited amount of time, I was able to get everything done and looking great! A nice addition this year was two moving lights, giving a lot more flexibility to craft the weird and fanatical world of Seuss that the director was looking for. The color palette was equally interesting, with more saturate and wacky colors used throughout to make it feel more fun!

Role: Lighting Designer
